International Borders

and why they are dumb.

.         .         .         .         .         .         .         .         .         .         .         .         .         .         .

            International borders are not natural creations. They are not a result of evolution, movement of tectonic plates, changes in climatic patterns, etc. The idea of where a nation ends and begins, and what a country is even, is an idea created by human society so as to maintain some form of order in the chaotic world. It establishes an easy, politically-correct (all though, this could be argued against) way for humanity to categorize and differentiate its members.

            The way weâ€™ve created borders is seemingly arbitrary. For example, if a border is made between nations at a riverbank, how does someone on one side of a river differ biologically, psychologically, physiologically, and chemically from someone on the other side? These border definitions have brought about differences over time in people groups on either side of the river as they are now separated legally, and, sometimes, culturally and linguistically.

            Borders between nations can be defined in a myriad of ways. Some borders are made using geographic features such as mountains or rivers. Others are made based on boundaries of historical kingdoms. Some borders are decided by a colonial power exiting or entering the region, or the country itself.

            In the short-run, the decision to form borders from such procedures may seem legitimate. However, over years physical features can change and people can move around, removing previously existing linguistic and religious barriers. These borders then appear arbitrary in the long-run. They leave communities divided, regions in turmoil, and economies in limbo.

            It is also important to consider maritime boundaries of a country created by international law. These are particularly important for island nations in the Pacific and the Caribbean. Any disagreement could be a significant hindrance to the growth and power of these states.
